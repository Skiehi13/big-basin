Who I’m talking to (and where)

This explains my target audience and why I picked these platforms for the post on the left.

My target audience is Californians living in fire-prone areas like Roseville, the Santa Cruz Mountains, Sierra foothills, and the LA outskirts. These are people who’ve packed go-bags, watched evacuation zones creep closer, or spent weeks checking AQI before opening a window. They may not know fire ecology jargon, but they know exactly what an orange sky feels like.

I’d share this post on X and Instagram. X is where people follow real-time fire maps, CAL FIRE updates, and local threads during fire season, so a tight, emotional hook about Big Basin and “the one thing that might save what’s left” fits the feed. Instagram, meanwhile, is built for the kind of post-fire forest image that stops a thumb mid-scroll. Pairing the photo with a short, raw caption lets the story of Tiffany, Big Basin, and prescribed fire land in a visual way, then the link in bio or story swipe takes people to the full story.
